## 7. KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR FRUTTÉ

1. **Total addressable market (TAM):** ~USD $447.5M (Colombian fruit pulp) but the pasteurized segment is only ~$22-36M currently — tiny but growing fast

2. **Serviceable addressable market (SAM):** The real play is HoReCa (132,000 establishments) + tiendas (450-500K) that can't handle frozen = **580,000+ potential clients** that frozen competitors can't efficiently serve

3. **Nutrium is the gorilla** at COP $264B revenue — but they're diversified across 10+ categories and focused on mango/export. They're not targeting Bogotá tiendas with shelf-stable pulp.

4. **Alimentos SAS is the pioneer** in aseptic pulp (since 2012) with 135 employees and 900-1200 tons/month capacity. They're the closest comp but showing revenue decline.

5. **CI Agrofrut (Canoa)** at USD $11.2M is a strong #2 in pure pulp — but focused on frozen + export.

6. **The pasteurized "larga vida" niche** has <10 players total: Alimentos SAS, Frutysabor, and a handful of others. This is still blue ocean compared to 34,000+ frozen producers.

7. **Export is booming** — 79% growth in pulp exports. Shelf-stable (aseptic) product ships in dry containers at ~50% lower logistics cost vs frozen.

8. **Tiendas de barrio are in crisis** (-3.6% sales Q1 2025) — they need new revenue categories. Shelf-stable pulp is a product they literally can't offer today due to freezer constraints.
